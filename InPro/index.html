The Challenge

Contents claims are only as good as the inventory behind them.

Incomplete, inconsistent, or unstructured inventories delay settlement, create costly revision loops, and expose carriers to disputes that reach the courts.

Incomplete & Vague Data

Contractor lists with grouped items, missing descriptions, and unclear quantities create gaps that stall carrier review and delay settlement.

Inconsistent Valuation

No standard methodology leads to unsupported pricing, US-sourced replacements, and schedules that cannot withstand adjuster scrutiny or formal dispute.

Delayed Settlement

Revision loops between adjusters, contractors, and insureds extend cycle times and increase operational cost at every stage of the claim.

Why Accuracy Matters

When the inventory is wrong, the entire claim is wrong.

Real cases. Real consequences. Names and identifying details withheld.

Expert Witness — Fire Loss

Following a total-loss residential fire, a policyholder submitted a $1.3 million contents claim. The household's reported income was approximately $120,000 annually.

The inventory firm assigned to the file — one of Canada's largest — permitted the Schedule of Loss to be completed by the insured with minimal validation, and no material challenges were raised at the line-item level.

The claim progressed through adjustment and into litigation. InPro was retained as a technical expert to conduct an independent review. We identified material inconsistencies, quantified the variances, and delivered a fully documented, defensible analysis that materially influenced the outcome of the case.

Outcome

Irregularities documented. File successfully challenged. Carrier protected.

Desk Claim Cleanup — Water Loss

A restoration contractor submitted a Schedule of Loss for a significant water damage claim. The inventory contained fewer than 15 line items — grouped entries like "kitchen contents," "master bedroom items," and "electronics" — each assigned a single lump-sum value with no itemization, no sourcing, and no depreciation applied.

InPro was assigned the desk review. We separated the grouped entries into over 200 individual line items, applied Canada-first LKQ pricing to each, and structured the depreciation correctly by category. The resulting schedule was fully traceable, carrier-ready, and reduced the unsubstantiated portion of the claim significantly.

Outcome

200+ items properly itemized. Defensible pricing applied. File settled without dispute.

High-Value Item Analysis — Theft Claim

An insured submitted a theft claim that included $180,000 in electronics, jewellery, and collectibles. Items were listed by name only — no model numbers, no purchase documentation, and several described in terms that did not correspond to any verifiable product. The adjuster flagged the file and engaged InPro for a detailed review.

We researched each item using current Canadian retail sources, cross-referenced descriptions against known product lines, and identified entries that could not be substantiated at the claimed values. Approximately $133,000 in claims were either reduced or removed based on sourcing, comparable pricing, and item verification — leaving $47,000 in supportable, documented replacement costs.

Outcome

$133K in unsupported claims identified. Carrier liability reduced by 74%.
